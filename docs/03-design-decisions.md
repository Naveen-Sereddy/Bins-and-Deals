03 · Design Decisions

Design Strategy

The website was designed around the most common customer questions identified during stakeholder discussions and customer conversations.

Rather than creating a visually complex experience, the goal was to make information easy to find, easy to understand, and easy to act on.

The design prioritized clarity, trust, and accessibility.

⸻

Information Architecture

The website structure was intentionally organized around a customer’s decision-making process.

The content answers the following questions in order:

1. What is Bins & Deals?
2. How does pricing work?
3. What products are available?
4. Where is the store located?
5. What are the store hours?
6. How can I contact the business?

This structure reduced the amount of searching required and ensured visitors could quickly find important information.

⸻

Pricing-First Experience

Research revealed that pricing was one of the strongest drivers of customer interest.

As a result:

* Pricing information was placed near the top of the experience.
* Visual pricing references were incorporated.
* The treasure-hunt model was explained clearly.
* Price expectations were established early.

This reduced uncertainty for first-time visitors.

⸻

Mobile-First Design

A large percentage of visitors discover the business through social media channels.

For this reason, the experience was designed mobile-first.

Key considerations included:

* Large tap targets
* Clear call-to-action buttons
* Fast access to contact information
* Readable typography
* Simplified navigation
* Responsive layouts across device sizes

⸻

Trust Building

Several design decisions were made to increase credibility and trust.

These included:

* Clear business information
* Consistent branding
* Embedded Google Maps integration
* Visible store hours
* Prominent contact information
* Facebook integration
* Marketplace integration

These elements help reassure visitors that the business is legitimate and active.

⸻

Supporting Existing Customer Behavior

Research indicated that Facebook already played a major role in customer engagement.

Instead of replacing that workflow, the website was designed to strengthen it.

Customers can easily:

* Visit the Facebook page
* Browse Marketplace listings
* Contact the store directly
* Get directions

This approach supports existing customer habits while improving the overall digital experience.

⸻

Technical Decisions

The project was built using React and Vite to provide a fast and maintainable foundation.

Key technical goals included:

* Fast page load times
* Responsive performance
* Component-based architecture
* Easy future updates
* SEO-friendly structure

The result is a scalable website that can continue evolving alongside the business.
