05 · UX Case Study

Bins & Deals

Designing a Digital Experience for a Local Liquidation Retail Business

⸻

Overview

Bins & Deals is a liquidation retail store located in Liberty, Missouri.

The business specializes in selling liquidation, overstock, returned, and surplus inventory at significantly discounted prices. Inventory changes frequently, creating a treasure-hunt shopping experience where customers regularly return to discover new deals.

I was hired directly by the business owner to help establish a professional online presence and improve how customers discover and understand the business.

The project involved research, design, development, deployment, SEO implementation, and ongoing support.

⸻

⸻

The Problem

The business had a strong local presence but lacked a dedicated website.

Customers frequently relied on Facebook posts, word-of-mouth referrals, or direct phone calls to gather information.

Several challenges emerged:

* Customers did not always understand the pricing model.
* Store information was scattered across multiple channels.
* New visitors lacked a clear understanding of available products.
* Important information required unnecessary effort to locate.
* The business needed a more professional online presence.

⸻

Research

Stakeholder Research

I worked directly with the owner to understand:

* Business goals
* Customer behavior
* Common questions
* Marketing channels
* Growth opportunities

Customer Conversations

I also spoke with customers visiting the store to understand what information mattered most before making a visit.

Several recurring themes emerged.

Pricing Clarity

Customers wanted pricing information immediately.

Product Expectations

Customers wanted to know what types of products were available before visiting.

Convenience

Store hours, address information, and contact details were among the most requested pieces of information.

Social Media Usage

Many customers already interacted with the business through Facebook and Marketplace listings.

⸻

Key Insights

The research revealed five primary opportunities.

1. Lead With Pricing

Pricing was one of the strongest motivators for customer interest.

2. Showcase Product Categories

Customers wanted confidence that the store carried products relevant to them.

3. Improve Information Accessibility

Store information should be accessible within seconds.

4. Support Existing Behavior

The website should complement Facebook rather than replace it.

5. Prioritize Mobile Users

Many visitors arrive through social media on mobile devices.

⸻

Design Process

The website structure was built around the questions customers most frequently asked.

Information Hierarchy

1. Store Introduction
2. Pricing System
3. Product Categories
4. Social Proof
5. Location Information
6. Store Hours
7. Contact Information

This structure ensures customers can quickly find the information they need.

⸻

Design Decisions

Pricing First

The pricing section was positioned prominently to reduce uncertainty and increase engagement.

Mobile First

The experience was optimized for smartphones using responsive layouts, large tap targets, and simplified navigation.

Trust Signals

The design incorporated:

* Store location
* Contact information
* Google Maps
* Store hours
* Facebook integration
* Marketplace integration

Clear Calls To Action

Phone calls, directions, and social channels were surfaced throughout the experience.

⸻

Development

The website was built using:

* React
* Vite
* JavaScript
* Tailwind CSS
* Framer Motion
* Lucide React

The architecture focused on performance, maintainability, and future scalability.

⸻

Launch

After stakeholder review and testing, the website was deployed as the primary online presence for the business.

The website continues to support customer discovery and communication.

Lessons Learned

This project reinforced the importance of speaking directly with both stakeholders and customers before designing solutions.

The most impactful improvements were not visual. They came from understanding what information customers needed, when they needed it, and how quickly they could access it.

Working with a real business provided valuable experience translating customer insights into practical design and development decisions that delivered measurable value.
