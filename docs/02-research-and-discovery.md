02 · Research & Discovery

Project Approach

This was a real client project completed for a local liquidation retail business. The discovery phase focused on understanding both business goals and customer behavior before making design decisions.

I worked directly with the owner to understand how customers discovered the store, what questions they frequently asked, and which business challenges could be solved through a website experience.

⸻

Stakeholder Interviews

During conversations with the owner, several recurring challenges emerged.

Challenge 1: Explaining the Store Concept

Many first-time visitors were unfamiliar with liquidation stores and did not fully understand how the business operated.

Customers often needed additional explanation regarding:

* Bin pricing
* Inventory sourcing
* Product availability
* Weekly inventory rotation

Challenge 2: Repetitive Customer Questions

The owner frequently received questions regarding:

* Store hours
* Location
* Product categories
* Pricing structure
* New inventory arrivals

Challenge 3: Digital Presence

The business relied heavily on Facebook and local word-of-mouth marketing. While these channels generated traffic, there was no dedicated website serving as a central source of information.

⸻

Customer Conversations

To better understand customer needs, I spoke informally with customers visiting the store.

Rather than conducting formal usability testing, these conversations focused on identifying what information customers wanted before deciding to visit.

Several patterns emerged consistently.

Customers Wanted Pricing Clarity

Many customers wanted to quickly understand how pricing worked before making a trip to the store.

The treasure-hunt pricing model was attractive, but it was not immediately obvious to first-time visitors.

Customers Wanted Product Expectations

Customers wanted to know:

* What types of products were available
* Whether electronics were sold
* Whether household goods were available
* If inventory changed frequently

Customers Wanted Convenience

Store location, hours, and contact information were among the most requested pieces of information.

Customers wanted these details immediately accessible without searching through multiple social media posts.

Customers Followed Social Channels

Many customers already engaged with the business through Facebook and Marketplace listings.

This indicated that the website should support existing customer behavior rather than replace it.

⸻

Research Insights

The research phase produced several key insights.

Insight 1

Pricing information should be visible immediately.

Insight 2

Product categories should be presented clearly to set expectations.

Insight 3

Store hours, address, and contact information should be highly visible throughout the experience.

Insight 4

The website should complement Facebook rather than compete with it.

Insight 5

The experience should be optimized for mobile devices because a significant portion of traffic originates from social media.

⸻

Success Criteria

The final experience would be considered successful if it could:

* Clearly explain the business model
* Reduce customer confusion
* Increase trust among new visitors
* Improve access to essential store information
* Support customer acquisition efforts
* Create a professional online presence for the business
